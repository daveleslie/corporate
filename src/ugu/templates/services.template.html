SMART METERING

The cornerstone of any effective energy management program is the ability to accurately measure business energy consumption. It is impossible to effectively manage what you can’t measure, and with this in mind, Ugu Engineering Solutions has designed and developed a powerful cloud-based enterprise energy management application. Unlike other smart metering software, it is designed to be easy to use for non-technical persons while still providing access to powerful information. The custom tools and reports of the system assist with all aspects of an integrated energy management program. The software is also cloud-based which gives users access to energy information from anywhere, and avoids the hassles of installation on different devices and compatibility issues.


Real-time energy monitoring

All Ugu systems are real-time interacting with live data streams from devices in the field. This enables an instantaneous assessment of store energy use at any time. Ugu’s online energy management system provides easy to understand graphical displays of energy usage with advanced graphical tools allowing deeper analysis and understanding of energy use patterns.



Graphical analysis tools

A set of graphing tools allows different ways to view energy information for each meter site. Each view is designed to be easy to use and understand. The different graphs enable a deeper understanding of energy use patterns which can help identify abnormal and wasted energy use.



Automated exception reports

The costs for retailers arising due to wasted energy use (outside trading hours), can be surprisingly high. By continuously monitoring meter load profiles, automatic exception reports generated on a daily basis can alert management to inefficient and wasted energy use. This can help to cut down on energy costs due to faulty or ineffective switching mechanisms., and can also be very effective in complimenting an ongoing energy awareness and staff engagement program



Tariff analysis

Electricity can be purchased at one of a few alternate tariff structures designed for different consumption profiles. This means that some tariffs are more expensive than others depending on the consumpption profile of the store. By analysing the actual consumption data for each meter, the most economical tariff can be automatically ascertained and applied for.



Invoice audits

Auditing of electricity accounts can be easily handled by comparing monthly invoices to the actual consumption data recorded by Ugu remote meters. A specific billing comparison tool allows quick identification of discrepancies and provides meaningful information to assist with disputes. Having access to real-time consumption data also makes budgeting for electricity costs easier and more accurate.



Initiative tracking

Calculating the impact of energy saving initiatives can be a challenge. This is now made simple by using the Ugu online initiative tracking tool. The tool allows for comparison of energy usage either side of the specific implementation start and finish dates, and calculates the resulting impact on both energy consumption and costs. The results of all initiatives are stored on the system for future reference, and a summary report can be downloaded in spreadsheet format, useful for multi site initiatives.



Carbon Emissions

Carbon emissions resulting from energy consumption account for as much as 70% of the total carbon footprint in a retail business. Ugu online automatically calculates the carbon emissions resulting from metered energy use on a monthly basis,making this otherwise tedious process both more accurate and less time consuming.



Benchmarking

Ugu online has a number of different benchmarking metrics for which reports can be generated to support energy target setting as well as quickly identify abnormal usage patterns down to a granular level. This kind of data is also useful in internal awareness programs and can be displayed on Ugu display screens for promotional purposes.



Display Screens

All energy data from smart meters can be dsiplayed in a dashboard layout on large flatscreen TV’s. This is useful for both public and internal promotion of energy awareness.
